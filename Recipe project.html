How to Make Shrimp Scampi with Linguine!

We have tried different versions of this recipe. Tyler Florence's is the best so far: all the ingredients well balanced, the lemon not overpowering or too tart: probably the additional butter creates the velvety sauce. Delicious and will do again.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound linguini
  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 4 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, plus more for drizzling
  • 2 shallots, finely diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • Pinch of red pepper flakes, optional
  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped parsley leaves

Directions

  1. Bring a large pot of water to boil and add a couple of tablespoons of salt and the linguini. Stir to make sure the pasta separates; cover.
  2. When the water returns to a boil cook for about 6 to 8 minutes.
  3. Drain the pasta and set aside.
  4. Meanwhile, in a large skillet, melt 2 tablespoons of butter and 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium-high heat.
  5. Saute the shallots, garlic, and red pepper flakes (if using) until the shallots are translucent. This takes approximately 3 to 4 minutes.
  6. After you've seasoned the shrimp with salt and pepper, add them to the pan and cook until they have turned pink. This takes approximately 2 to 3 minutes. Remove the shrimp from the pan and set aside to keep warm.
  7. In that now emptied pan, add the white wine and lemon juice and bring to a boil.
  8. Afterwards, add 2 tablespoons of butter and 2 tablespoons of oil.
  9. When the butter has melted, return the shrimp to the pan along with the cooked pasta and parsley.
  10. Stir well and season with salt and pepper. Drizzle a bit of olive oil overtop and serve immediately!

Prep time: 15 minutes

Cook time: 25 minutes

Total time: 40 minutes
